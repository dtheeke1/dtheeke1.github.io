About the Artist

David Theeke

Since childhood I've had a fascination with tattoos. I remember being mesmerized by my Uncle's sleeves at a very early age. Growing up, I never imagined that making a living as a tattoo artist was even possible.

In the late 80s I met Rob Jensen, who turned out to be one of my closest friends and mentors. It was in 1995 Rob convinced me to combine my love for art, tattoos and my business experience and we became business partners, opening Artrageous Ink in late '95. Rob apprenticed me as a tattoo artist and I taught him the business side. After a few great years, Rob moved down south and I bought out his share.

I spend the majority of my tattooing time doing larger scale custom work. I strive towards being a versatile artist and creating whatever my clients can conjure up in their imaginations. I do however have a great fondness and appreciation for Asian style tattoos.

Healing Your Tattoo

Aftercare Instructions

1

Leave your bandage on overnight.

2

Remove the bandage and wash your tattoo with a mild liquid soap and warm water. Wash gently with your hand — do not use a towel or anything abrasive. Remove all dried blood and secretions. Rinse and pat dry with a towel.

3

Apply a small amount of A&D ointment, massage it in, and wipe off any excess with your hand. Do not leave a film — a tattoo needs air to heal.

4

Repeat the washing and ointment process twice a day for three days.

5

After three days, discontinue A&D ointment. You can still get your tattoo wet in the shower, but stop scrubbing it. Switch to a small amount of unscented white hand lotion (Lubriderm or Curell) — massage in until absorbed, twice a day. No perfumes or dyes.

6

Avoid soaking, chlorine, and direct sunlight for two weeks. Getting it wet briefly is fine; prolonged soaking is not.

7

Do not pick or scratch. Whatever flakes off during a gentle wash is fine — do not force flakes off before they are ready. Doing so may cause color loss.

8

If clothing sticks to your tattoo, do not pull it off. Wet the clothing until it falls away on its own. Loose clothing over the tattoo is fine; tight or restrictive clothing is not.

9

Use sunblock to maintain the quality of a healed tattoo.

Failure to follow these instructions can extend healing time or adversely affect the quality of your tattoo.
